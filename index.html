Malaysia Weather Dashboard

This dashboard provides a concise view of Malaysia's weather patterns in 2025. Explore precipitation, humidity, wind speed, cloudcover, visibility, solar radiation and moonphase by month.

Precipitation vs Humidity

Scatter interpretation

The scatterplot shows most observations clustered at low precipitation (< 0.5 in) with relatively high humidity (~70–85%), indicating many humid days without heavy rain. Larger precipitation events are rare but occur alongside high humidity (e.g. Apr shows the largest rain point ≈3.2 in). Wind-speed (point size) does not show a clear positive relationship with precipitation magnitude — larger wind points appear across low-to-moderate rainfall values. Overall, humidity tends to be high when rainfall occurs, but high humidity alone does not guarantee heavy precipitation.

Monthly Averages (small multiples)

Bar charts interpretation

Average cloudcover peaks in Apr (~40–43%) and is lowest in Oct (~28%), suggesting April is the cloudiest month while October is relatively clearer. Visibility is stable (~5.8–6.2 km) with slightly higher values in Oct and Feb. Solar radiation rises from January (Jan) toward a peak in Oct, matching the lower cloudcover in October. Moonphase averages vary little month-to-month; Feb is marginally higher. These monthly patterns suggest October is the clearest/sunniest month and April has both higher cloud cover and notable heavy-rain events.
