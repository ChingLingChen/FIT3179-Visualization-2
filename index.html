Malaysia Weather Dashboard

This dashboard presents Malaysia's weather trends.
Interactive charts help reveal seasonal patterns.

Hourly Temperature by Malaysian State

At 12 PM, inland states show elevated temperatures, often exceeding 28°C. Coastal and highland regions remain relatively cooler, around 25°C.

Evening hours (e.g., 8 PM) reveal a cooling trend across most regions; East Malaysia shows more stable temperatures compared to the peninsula.

Weather Relationships and Monthly Averages (2025)

    

    

Main pattern
Most data points show low precipitation (< 0.5 in) with high humidity (~70–85%), typical of humid tropical days with light or no rain.

Extreme events
Heavy rainfall is rare but notable — e.g., April 23rd recorded ~3.2 in. These events align with very high humidity.

Wind relationship
Wind speed (point size) shows no clear link to rainfall. Strong winds occur across low and moderate rain levels.

Cloudcover
Cloud cover peaks in April (~40–43%) and dips in October (~28%), matching rainfall trends.

Visibility
Visibility stays steady (~5.8–6.2 km), slightly clearer in October and February.

Solar radiation
Radiation rises from January to a peak in October, reflecting reduced cloudcover.

Moonphase & seasonality
Moonphase shows little variation. Seasonal signals are clearer in precipitation, cloudcover, and solar radiation.

Monthly Weather Metrics (2025)

Precipitation peaks in April, drops mid-year, then rises toward the end of the year.

Humidity stays high year-round with a slight dip mid-year.

Wind speed increases early-year then stabilizes.

UV index is elevated and peaks near October.
