Hourly Temperature by Malaysian State

Daytime temperatures are generally higher in inland regions such as Kedah and Perak, with values often exceeding 35°C. Coastal states like Sabah and Sarawak tend to remain cooler, especially during early morning and late evening hours, often dropping below 25°C.

Temperature peaks typically occur between 12 PM and 3 PM, while the lowest readings are observed around dawn. The east coast shows more pronounced daily fluctuations compared to the central peninsula.

Monthly Weather Metrics (2025)

Precipitation peaks in April, then drops sharply through mid-year, reaching its lowest in July. From August onward, rainfall gradually increases, suggesting a transition toward wetter conditions.

Humidity remains consistently high, with slight declines from May to July and a recovery in August and September. Wind speed rises steadily from January to April, then stabilizes at moderate levels. UV index stays elevated throughout the year, peaking in October, indicating strong solar exposure even beyond the dry season.
