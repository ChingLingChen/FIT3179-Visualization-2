Malaysia Weather Dashboard

This dashboard provides a comprehensive overview of Malaysia's weather patterns through a series of interactive charts and maps. Users can explore key meteorological metrics such as precipitation, humidity, wind speed, and UV index across different months and regions.

Hourly Temperature by Malaysian State

At 12 PM, inland states show elevated temperatures, often exceeding 28°C. In contrast, coastal and highland regions remain relatively cooler, hovering around 25°C.

Evening hours (e.g., 8 PM) reveal a noticeable cooling trend across most regions, with East Malaysia maintaining more stable temperatures compared to the fluctuating heat in the central peninsula.

Weather Relationships and Monthly Averages (2025)

Scatter Plot Trends: The scatter plot reveals a strong positive correlation between precipitation and humidity in 2025, with higher precipitation levels (April and June) corresponding to increased humidity. Notably, January shows moderate precipitation with lower humidity, while October exhibits a wider spread, suggesting variable weather conditions. Wind speed influences the scatter size, with larger circles in March indicating stronger winds.

Bar Charts Trends: Monthly averages for cloud cover, visibility, solar radiation, and moonphase show distinct patterns. Cloud cover peaks in April and June, reducing visibility, while solar radiation is highest in January and February. Moonphase averages remain stable, with slight increases in September and October, reflecting lunar cycle influences.

Monthly Weather Metrics (2025)

Precipitation peaks in April, then drops sharply through mid-year, reaching its lowest in July. From August onward, rainfall gradually increases, suggesting a transition toward wetter conditions.

Humidity remains consistently high, with slight declines from May to July and a recovery in August and September. Wind speed rises steadily from January to April, then stabilizes at moderate levels. UV index stays elevated throughout the year, peaking in October, indicating strong solar exposure even beyond the dry season.
